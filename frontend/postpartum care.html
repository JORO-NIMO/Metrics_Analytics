Mother's Recovery

🩸 Bleeding 💊 Pain 🧵 Stitches 🔪 C-section 🚨 Danger Signs

🩸 Bleeding After Birth (Lochia)

Days 1-3:

  • Bright red blood
  • Like heavy period
  • Small clots (grape-sized)
  • Change pad every 2-3 hours

Days 4-10:

  • Pinkish-brown
  • Lighter flow
  • Change pad 3-4 times/day

Days 11-14+:

  • Whitish-yellow
  • Spotting only
  • May last up to 6 weeks

🚨 SEE DOCTOR IF:

  • Soaking 1 pad per hour
  • Clots bigger than your fist
  • Bleeding turns bright red again
  • Foul smell (infection)
  • Severe pain

💊 Pain Management

Normal Pains:

  • Afterpains (cramping)
  • Worse when breastfeeding
  • Lasts 2-3 days

What Helps:

  • Paracetamol (if prescribed)
  • Warm compress on belly
  • Empty bladder often
  • Gentle massage

🧵 Perineal Care (If you had stitches)

✅ DO:

  • Keep area clean
  • Wash with clean water after toilet
  • Pat dry (don't rub)
  • Change pads often

❌ DON'T:

  • Use soap inside
  • Sit for long periods
  • Strain during bowel movements

🔪 C-Section Recovery

  • Keep incision clean and dry
  • No heavy lifting (nothing heavier than baby)
  • No driving for 2 weeks
  • No sex for 6 weeks
  • Gentle walking is good

🚨 SEE DOCTOR IF:

  • Incision opens
  • Pus or foul smell
  • Fever

🚑 CALL DOCTOR IMMEDIATELY IF:

  • Heavy bleeding (soaking 1 pad per hour)
  • Fever >38°C
  • Severe headache
  • Vision changes
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Chest pain
  • Swollen, painful leg
  • Thoughts of hurting yourself or baby

Breastfeeding Guide

How to Breastfeed

  1. Get comfortable - Sit with back support, use pillows
  2. Position baby - Tummy to tummy, nose to nipple
  3. Help baby latch - Wait for wide mouth, bring to breast

✅ Good latch signs:

  • Mouth wide open
  • Lips curled out (like fish)
  • Chin touching breast
  • No pain (just tugging)
  • You hear swallowing

✗ Poor latch signs:

  • Painful
  • Clicking sounds
  • Baby's cheeks dimple
  • Nipple flattened after

Is Baby Getting Enough Milk?

Age Wet Diapers
Day 1-2 1-2 wet diapers
Day 3-4 3-4 wet diapers
Day 5+ 6-8 wet diapers

⚠️ Signs baby is NOT getting enough:

  • Fewer than 6 wet diapers after day 5
  • Dark urine
  • Always fussy/crying
  • Not gaining weight
  • Very sleepy, hard to wake

Common Problems

😖 SORE NIPPLES

Fix: Improve latch, express milk on nipples, try different positions

🍼 ENGORGEMENT (Very full breasts)

Fix: Feed more often, warm compress before feeding, cold pack between feeds

🔴 MASTITIS (Infected breast)

Signs: Red, painful, hot area; fever

Fix: KEEP FEEDING! See doctor - may need antibiotics

Newborn Care (0-6 weeks)

🛁 Bathing Your Baby

How often: 2-3 times/week. Sponge bath until cord falls off.

Step by step:

  1. Keep baby warm - only undress part you're washing
  2. Wash face first (no soap)
  3. Wash body, gentle in folds
  4. Pat dry (don't rub)
  5. Put on clean diaper, clothes

⚠️ SAFETY: NEVER leave baby alone in water!

🧼 Umbilical Cord Care

✅ DO:

  • Keep clean and dry
  • Fold diaper below cord
  • Let air get to it
  • Let it fall off naturally (5-15 days)

❌ DON'T:

  • Put anything on it (no herbs, no oil)
  • Cover tightly
  • Pull it off

🚨 Signs of infection (SEE DOCTOR):

  • Redness around base
  • Pus or foul smell
  • Swelling
  • Bleeding
  • Baby has fever

😴 Safe Sleep

  • Always place baby on BACK
  • Firm mattress, no pillows
  • No soft toys or loose blankets
  • Baby in same room (not same bed)
  • No smoking around baby

🚑 DANGER SIGNS IN NEWBORN:

  • Fever >38°C
  • Too cold (below 36°C)
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Refuses to feed
  • Vomiting (not just spit-up)
  • Hard to wake
  • Fewer than 4 wet diapers
  • Yellow skin/eyes (jaundice)

Mental Health After Birth

You are not alone

1 in 7 mothers experience postpartum depression. It's an illness, NOT a weakness. It's treatable.

Signs (for 2+ weeks):

  • Feeling sad, hopeless, overwhelmed
  • Can't sleep (even when baby sleeps)
  • Sleeping too much
  • Not eating or eating too much
  • Irritable, angry
  • Extreme anxiety, panic attacks
  • No bond with baby
  • Thoughts of hurting yourself or baby
